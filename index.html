Can AI Ever Be Truly Fair? The Hidden Bias in Our Algorithms


In this article, I explore how bias quietly seeps into artificial intelligence — not because algorithms are inherently flawed, but because they learn from the imperfect world we’ve built. From facial recognition inaccuracies to biased recruitment systems, I reveal how human assumptions get encoded into data and decisions.

I also discuss how achieving fairness in AI goes beyond technical fixes — it requires empathy, accountability, and ethical awareness. This piece reflects on the shared responsibility between data scientists, engineers, and society to ensure that intelligence — artificial or otherwise — remains just and humane.

The Silent Power of Missing Data


In the world of data science, missing values are often seen as a nuisance — something to fix or remove. But what if they’re actually trying to tell us something? In this article, I explore how missing data can reveal hidden patterns, biases, and business insights that most analysts overlook.

From healthcare to finance, the absence of data often carries meaning — signaling risk, behavior, or system inefficiency. I also discuss smart imputation strategies, anomaly detection, and ethical considerations around data gaps.

This piece encourages data scientists to stop treating missing data as noise — and start seeing it as a quiet messenger of deeper truths.
